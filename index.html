Biography

About

A research path shaped by hearing science, human perception, and the tools that make careful experiments possible.

I have a passion for understanding human hearing and for using technology to support people who experience deafness or hearing loss.

In 2021, I was awarded fellowships from the Belgian American Educational Foundation and FWO to study under Dr. Tom Francart and Dr. Astrid van Wieringen in the experimental Oto-Rhino-Laryngology laboratory at KU Leuven in Belgium.

Before that, I obtained my Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Rochester with a research focus in auditory neuroscience. During my master's work, I independently conducted a study on the effects of audiovisual congruence on the auditory brainstem response to speech.

I also have earlier research experience studying the inner ear and the cochlea through Optical Coherence Tomography at Texas A&M University.

Today, my work continues to connect neuroscience, engineering, and interaction design in ways that make difficult human questions easier to study.
